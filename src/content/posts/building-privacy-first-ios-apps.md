---
title: Building Privacy-First iOS Apps
description: Why I prefer building simple, local-first iOS apps that respect user privacy and avoid unnecessary accounts, tracking, and cloud dependency.
date: 2026-01-01
category: iOS Development
---

Modern apps often ask for too much too early: accounts, tracking, analytics, cloud sync, and permissions before the user has even received value.

For many utility apps, a better default is simple: keep data on the device, make the core workflow fast, and only add cloud features when they clearly help the user.

This is the approach I try to take with my own iOS apps. Driver Logbook, Five Prayers, Aussie Loan Calculator, and FreezerPal are all based around practical daily use, not unnecessary data collection.

A privacy-first app does not need to feel limited. It can still be polished, useful, and reliable. The difference is that the app respects the user by default.
